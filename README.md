# Digital Twin of Slovenia (DTSI)

[![License: MIT](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-yellow.svg)](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)

## Overview

The Digital Twin of Slovenia (DTSI) is an ambitious project aimed at creating a comprehensive digital representation of Slovenia's physical and environmental characteristics. By leveraging Earth observation data, geospatial technologies, and advanced modeling techniques, DTSI provides a virtual replica of Slovenia's landscape, infrastructure, and natural resources.

## About

A digital twin is a virtual model designed to accurately reflect a physical object, system, or environment. The Digital Twin of Slovenia serves as a dynamic, data-driven platform that integrates multiple data sources to enable:

- **Real-time monitoring** of environmental conditions
- **Predictive analysis** for planning and decision-making
- **Scenario simulation** for impact assessment
- **Data visualization** of Slovenia's geographic and environmental data

## Use Cases

The Digital Twin of Slovenia can support various applications:

- **Environmental Monitoring**: Track changes in land use, vegetation, and natural resources
- **Urban Planning**: Assist in sustainable development and infrastructure planning
- **Disaster Management**: Support emergency response and risk assessment
- **Climate Analysis**: Study climate patterns and environmental changes
- **Resource Management**: Optimize management of natural resources
- **Policy Making**: Provide data-driven insights for governmental decisions
